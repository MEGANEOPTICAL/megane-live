The origin

Learn about our glasses origin.

福井 鯖江 Fukui Sabae

A small city in Fukui prefecture makes most of Japan's eyewear, and has done for over a century. Frames there pass through dozens of specialist hands — cutting, curing, polishing, assembly — before they are ever worn.

We visit the workshops, meet the makers, and bring back only the frames we would wear ourselves. No wholesale catalogues. No mass-market lines.

純チタン

Pure titanium

Light, hypoallergenic and precise — the material Sabae is famous for, cold-forged for strength without weight.

生地

Japanese acetate

Slow-cured cellulose acetate with depth of colour you can't get from injection-moulded plastic.

手仕事

Hand finishing

Barrel-tumbled and hand-polished over days, so edges sit softly against the skin and hold their lustre.

Questions

Before you visit.

Can I use my health fund optical extras?
Yes — frames and prescription lenses are claimable under most optical extras cover. Bring your health fund details and we'll help you with the claim in store.
Do I need an appointment to try frames?
Walk-ins are welcome for browsing. For an eye test or a dedicated styling session, booking ahead guarantees you one-on-one time.
Can you fit lenses to my existing prescription?
Yes. Bring a prescription less than two years old, or book an eye test with us and we'll take care of everything in one visit.
What if my frame breaks or loosens?
Adjustments and servicing are free for life on every frame we sell — and our Japanese makers support their frames with genuine replacement parts.
Are all frames really made in Japan?
Every frame in store is made in Japan, most in Sabae, Fukui. No diffusion lines, no "designed in Japan" workarounds.

Eyecare

Eye tests, done properly.

Across Australia, eye tests keep getting shorter. Industry surveys now put the median consultation at 30 minutes and falling, with optometrists increasingly measured on sales conversion instead of care.

MEGANE is independent, so we answer to nobody's sales dashboard. Our optometrist carries no conversion targets, consultations run 45 minutes as standard, and recalls follow clinical need — nothing else. The only KPI in the room is your eyesight.

Care here is tailored, not templated. We test around how you actually use your eyes — screens, night driving, fine work, sport — then walk you through what we found and what it means, in plain language, with time for questions. You leave knowing every option and what each one costs, so the decision is genuinely yours. Sometimes the right answer is new glasses. Sometimes it isn't — and we'll tell you either way.

Members receive annual comprehensive testing, priority appointments and preferred pricing on lenses and frames.

    About

    MEGANE means glasses.
    That's the whole point.

    No invented heritage, no borrowed Italian name. めがね is simply the Japanese word for spectacles — and everything we sell is simply, entirely, made in Japan.

    誠実な仕事

    MEGANE started with a frustration: the best Japanese frames rarely leave Japan, and when they do, they're marked up and fitted carelessly. We wanted a place in Melbourne where those frames are treated the way they're made — with patience.

    So we keep the range small. We travel to Fukui to choose it. We fit every pair in person, and we service what we sell for life. If that sounds slow, it is. Good glasses are slow.

    The store opens in August 2026. Eye test bookings are open now, and the first collection is available online.

    よい眼鏡は、かける人の毎日を静かに支える。 Good glasses quietly carry their wearer through every day.
